The Story

Who I Am.

I'm a thesis-based MASc researcher at Concordia University's DISCOS Lab, focused on distributed consensus protocols and fault-tolerant systems algorithms designed to stay correct when machines fail or behave maliciously.

My thesis work on the CORA Protocol explores object weighted consensus in heterogeneous clusters. I design systems in Go, evaluate them rigorously, and translate research into production-ready code. Beyond that. I've developed full-stack applications with React/Node.js, AI systems with PyTorch, and blockchain solutions on Hyperledger Fabric.

"Build systems that stay correct under failure."

Before grad school, I earned my Bachelor of Engineering in Information Technology and worked in technical support and AI/ML data quality. That foundation shaped how I think about reliability, systems design, and shipping software that works in the real world.
